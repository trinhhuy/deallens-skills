# Interview Lens

When the user's context indicates they're researching a company for a job interview, adapt the brief with these adjustments.

## Sections to emphasize

### Replace "Recent signals" with "Signals for candidates"

Focus signals on what matters to a candidate:

- **Growth signals** (good for candidate): hiring surge, expansion, funding
- **Stability signals** (good): consistent leadership, profitability mentions, established customer base
- **Warning signals** (be aware): layoffs, repeated leadership departures, missed earnings, negative Glassdoor trends, hiring freeze mentions
- **Culture signals**: engineering blog activity, conference talks, open source contributions, employee tweets

For each, frame implications: "Hiring 50 engineers in Q3 → growing fast, expect chaos but also opportunity"

### Replace "Key people" with "People you might work with"

Focus on:
- The hiring manager's manager (if you can find role from job listing)
- The function's senior leader (e.g., VP Engineering if you're a developer)
- The CEO (always relevant)
- Recent thought leadership from people in your function (blog posts, talks)

Include their:
- Background and tenure
- Recent public posts (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Conference talks or articles they've written
- LinkedIn URL

This gives the candidate conversation hooks for interviews.

### Add "Questions to ask in interviews" section

Based on the research, suggest 5-7 specific questions the candidate should ask:

- Questions about specific recent events (signal-based)
- Questions about strategy/direction
- Questions about the team and ways of working
- Questions that show research without being suck-up

Example: If you detected they just opened a London office, suggest: "I noticed you opened the London office in October — is this team contributing to that effort, and what's the EU expansion thesis?"

### Add "Red flags to investigate" section

If signals suggest concerns, list them with how to probe in interview:

- Multiple leadership departures → "How has the company evolved its strategy through recent leadership changes?"
- Funding signals concerning → "Could you share more about the company's runway and path to profitability?"
- Public negative reviews → consult Glassdoor, ask culture questions

### Skip or downplay

- Direct competitive analysis (not relevant to candidate)
- Outreach/contact strategies (irrelevant — they have the interview)
- Pricing or revenue specifics (unless candidate is in finance role)

## Tone adjustments

- Use "you" addressing the candidate directly
- Empathetic — they're nervous about an interview
- Practical — focus on what they can act on

## Example transformation

Original signal: "🟢 **Funding** — Oct 2025. Closed $50M Series C led by Sequoia."

Interview lens: "🟢 **Healthy funding** — Oct 2025. Closed $50M Series C led by Sequoia. **For you as a candidate**: This means stability, likely runway 18-24 months, and they're hiring against a growth plan. Sequoia involvement suggests strong board. Ask: 'How does the Series C change priorities for this team?'"
